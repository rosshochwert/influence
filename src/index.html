    Power: Why Some People Have It - and Others Don't
    "As soon as you recognize the just-world effect and its influence on your perceptions and try to combat the tendency to see the world as inherently fair, you will be able to learn more in every situation and be more vigilant and proactive to ensure your own success."

    Jeffrey Pfeffer
    What is this?
    This is a personal project to help leverage Power and Influence tactics as I travel through my career.
    I am a dual-degree student at Penn, completing an MBA at the Wharton School and a MSE in Data Science. I interned this summer at Meta (Facebook) and am returning full-time this summer as a Data Scientist.
    Why a website?
    1. It seemed fun
    2. I like coding websites, and I want to keep my skills sharp
    3. A website can be ported anywhere (powerpoints more difficult to access on my phone)
    4. Robert Moses taught me to always use a diorama :)
    The Three Sections to This Site
    I've divided this project into three discrete sections:
    Action Plan:

    Using course concepts, I have developed a first year action plan (similar to first 100 day plan), where I've outlined what I hope to accomplish as I start work at Meta as a data scientist
    Key Concepts:

    This is my cheatsheet where I list the important concepts I learned, a few examples, and how I'd like to apply them at Meta
    Networking Tracker Concepts:

    This is a link to a google doc I created for networking purposes. It lists my major contacts, and when I last reached out to them. I considered building in some automated emailing system, but it felt inauthentic to me. I prefer to look over the full list and see who I need to reach out to.
    Why is this going to help me?
    Like most large organizations, Meta (Facebook) is incredibly political. A prime example is their ways of working: they do not use email, but rather an internal FB tool called Workplace. All work is posted publically, and judged not only by the impact, but by how many likes / shares / comments it received.

    Over the summer, I was very shy about sharing my work (believing in the just world hypothesis), but quickly learned you need to promote yourself to win. I consider this class and website to be great tools to help me!
