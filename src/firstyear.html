    My First Year Plan
    Concerning Things For Which Men, And Especially Princes, Are Praised Or Blamed
    "Therefore it is unnecessary for a prince to have all the good qualities I have enumerated, but it is very necessary to appear to have them. And I shall dare to say this also, that to have them and always to observe them is injurious, and that to appear to have them is useful"

    Context
    I am returning to Meta (Facebook) as a Data Scientist after my summer internship. Below, I outline a rough plan for me to cultivate power and influence in the organization.

    When I join, there is a critical 4-6 week process where I interview with different teams that have openings for my role. Much of work focuses on that period.

    I focus immediately on Meta as that is where I find these concepts most applicable, but of course these concepts can and should be applied throughout my life. Concepts are italicized

    First Week

    • Reach out to everyone I know from the somewhere (networks), see networking list
    • Sit near the kitchen, walk around the office; take advantage of the free time and chat with everyone (across teams, orgs, functions and products)
    • Go on workplace (internal FB tool), and find published research I find interesting; put time on the authors' calendar (agency) and learn more

    First Month

    • Take advantage of my power as a fresh grad, former intern looking to join a team.
    • Find places where I can win! Places that will leverage my unique MBA / MSE DS are best
    • Use my former intern manager as my hype-woman (persuasion) to try to network into a good team

    First Quarter

    • Perform! But also, make sure people know that I am performing well (agency). I want to be known as "the man who got things done" a la Robert Moses
    • Post on Workplace (FB internal tool), tag people and get likes / comments / shares.
    • Continue to network, chatting with everyone! Make it a goal to have lunch with someone new at least once a week. Should be easy in such a large org.

    First Half

    • Make the speech! Sign up to evangelize the good work I've been doing. It helps to network and build ethos!
    • Get feedback on my performance. How am I doing? Where can I improve? What do I need to do to put myself in a position for early promotion by year end?

    And Beyond

    At this point, I hope I have established a small foothold in the org. As I think about my long-term career, I need to do remember the following:
    • Network outside Facebook. I have many connections and I need to keep them fresh. Actively seek opportunities to replenish ops within the org and outside
    • Start recruiting for new roles. It seems silly, but my goal is to be in this role for 12-24 months. Having a pulse on the market is helpful
    Note: This is a student project for a class at Wharton School of Business called Power and Influence.
